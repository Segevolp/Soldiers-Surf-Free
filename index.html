About the project

A flexible platform built in memory of a friend who loved the sea.

Ido Aviv was head of surf coaching at the Kiryat Yam surf club. After he fell in the war, his family and his employer Gil Hurvitz set out to commemorate him in a way that fit who he was.

Ido used to hand his surfboard to any soldier who walked up. "Take a surfboard, I trust you." We built this system to let that gesture scale — across cities, beaches, and the years to come.

Soldiers Surf Free is an end-to-end platform: a flexible backend, a microcontroller-driven locker, an OCR pipeline that reads IDF cards, and a frontend that lives on Ido's memorial site. The platform is generic by design — different items, different sites, different cities can all plug in over time.

The project is being delivered with our customer Gil Hurvitz, as a 2025–2026 senior project at the Faculty of Engineering, Ben-Gurion University of the Negev.

Patent-grade ID auth

A patented OCR pipeline reads the soldier's IDF card and authenticates against an encrypted store before any locker opens.

Hardware ↔ cloud

RFID-triggered lockers talk to AWS IoT in real time. Door-close, presence, and telemetry all flow back into the system.

Built to scale

Multiple sites, lockers, item types, and tiered admin/site-manager permissions — designed for cities far beyond Kiryat Yam.

Geofenced policies

GPS distance, walk-in policy, daily rental caps, and reservation rules are all enforced server-side per site.

Manual

How to use the system.

Three paths: a soldier borrowing a board, a site manager keeping a beach running, an admin commissioning a new site.
1
Sign up & verify your IDF card

Open the Soldiers Surf Free tab on Ido Aviv's memorial site. Sign up with your phone, then upload a photo of your IDF card. Our OCR pipeline reads the card and stores an encrypted hash — never the raw image.

2
Pick a beach & reserve (optional)

Browse active sites. Some beaches require a reservation, others allow walk-ins — site policy is enforced server-side. Reservations have a grace period; no-shows free up the slot automatically.

3
Tap your card to unlock

Walk up to the locker. Tap your IDF card on the RFID reader. The system checks your documents, your bans, your reservation, and your GPS distance from the cabinet — all in one go. The right cell pops open.

4
Surf, return, leave a review

When you're back, tap the card again. The cell confirms the board is inside and the door has closed. The rental closes itself. Leave a review — it helps other soldiers and the site manager.

1
Manage your site

Site managers see a localized dashboard with active rentals, walk-ins, reservations, and the current state of every cell at the beach.

2
Resolve malfunctions

When a soldier reports a broken board, a stuck door or a faulty sensor, it lands in your queue. Categorize it (item / locker / cell / site / electrical), assign yourself, and resolve it from the field.

3
Remote unlock & reset

Need to override? Manager controls let you remotely unlock a cell, reset its state, or block it from rental. Every override is logged.

4
Set the rules

Walk-in policy, daily rental cap, reservation duration, max active reservations — all configurable per site. Rules are enforced server-side, every borrow attempt.

1
Provision a new site

Admins onboard a new beach: city, address, contacts, GPS coordinates, default policies. Then assign a site manager.

2
Add lockers, cells & items

Register a new physical locker, add cells to it, then drop items (surfboards or any other rentable type) into specific cells. Each item has condition, RFID code and lifecycle.

3
Manage permissions (tree-like)

Grant admin or site-manager permissions to other users. Permissions are hierarchical — admins can scope a site manager to specific sites only.

4
Bans, donations & oversight

Ban abusive users, review donations and receipts, audit user infractions and points. Override any field operation when the situation calls for it.
