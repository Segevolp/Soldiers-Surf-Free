01

Deployment Architecture

SINGLE BOX vs. 3-BOX STRATEGY
click any text to edit

This guide details the correct path from prototype to operational system in a marine environment. It includes a review of possible architectures, wiring maps, anti-corrosion sealing methods, and a complete purchase list.

In both methods, M25 connectors are mandatory for connecting the short "tails" of endpoint equipment (lock/RFID) to the longer field cables. The difference is in the location of cable and board concentration. The system is currently designed according to the 3-box method.

⚠ Key Decision

M25 connectors are required in BOTH architectures. The 3-box strategy was chosen for its clean wiring, flexibility, and lower cost — even though it requires more boxes and cable runs between them.

Parameter 3-Box Strategy CHOSEN 1-Box Strategy
Cable management Clean and easy. Each box handles one topic (power, sensors, brain). No spaghetti wiring. Very dense. Requires terminal blocks to split signals. Many grounds and voltages converge.
Build difficulty Medium. Requires building 3 boxes, running cables between them, and drilling multiple glands. Mechanically hard, logically easy. Fitting 10 glands in one box is challenging, but no inter-box cables.
Cost Depends on availability — likely cheaper. Hard to source, likely more expensive.
Flexibility & distance Maximum flexibility. Sensor box (limited to 2m from cells) can sit far from the brain box. Limited distance. The entire central box must be within 2m of all cells due to sensor cables.
02

3-Box Architecture

THE 3-BOX STRATEGY — CURRENT DESIGN

The system is divided into a brain box (primary) and secondary distribution boxes near the cell clusters.

🧠
BOX 1

Main Control Hub

  • ESP32 microcontroller
  • Main voltage converter (12V → 5V)
  • Logic converter (5V → 3.3V)
  • Receives main 12V, routes power & comms
  • Protected logic signals (3.3V from cable noise)
📦 134 × 134 × 66 mm
📡
BOX 2

Sensor Hub

  • 4× JSN-SR04T ultrasonic sensor boards
  • Hardware config: Mode 3 (R27 jumper or 470kΩ)
  • Binary output: occupied/free per cell
  • No trigger/echo wires — single signal line
  • Must be within 2m of all locker cells
  • Connected via 1× 8-wire cable from Box 1
📦 114 × 114 × 62 mm
⚡
BOX 3

Power & Relay Hub

  • 4-Channel relay module
  • Small voltage splitter (chocolate block)
  • Drives 4 solenoid locks + RFID reader
  • Cables exit toward devices — M25 field connectors sit near each device, not at the box
  • Connected via 2× 8-wire cables from Box 1
📦 114 × 114 × 62 mm
ℹ Cable Infrastructure

All inter-box cabling uses UL2464 8-Core 20AWG flexible cable — ideal for M25 glands (6.9mm diameter). Wire thickness matches terminal screw pitch exactly. Run cables inside conduit or through lockers for UV protection.

04

M25 Connector Tables

APPENDIX — LOCK & RFID WATERPROOF FIELD CONNECTORS

M25 5-pin waterproof submarine connectors are field connectors — located near each device, not at the box. Each device (lock or RFID reader) comes with short factory cables. The M25 acts as the IP68-rated junction point that splices the device's short cable onto the longer AWG20 8-core run that travels back to Box 3.

📍 Placement Rule

M25 connectors sit physically close to each lock or RFID reader — not at the enclosure. The long AWG20 cable runs from Box 3 → field → M25 junction → short device tail. For locks, only 4 of the 8 cable cores are used; the remaining 4 are trimmed inside the sheath.

Lock Connector · M25 5-Pin (4 of 5 pins used, 3 cable cores unused)

Pin 1 Red (+12V) +12V power wire Opening power
Pin 2 Black (GND) + White (NO) GND power wire Shared ground for lock and door sense
Pin 3 Green (Signal) Relay 1–4 wire Open trigger from relay
Pin 4 Yellow (COM) Sensor 1–4 feedback Returns to ESP32 (INPUT_PULLUP mode)
Pin 5 Blue (NC) Not connected Cut and insulate the blue wire
RFID Reader Connector · M25 5-Pin (all 5 pins used)
M25 Pin RFID Wire Side Main Cable Side Logic Notes
Pin 1 Red (+12V) Spliced from +12V Reader power
Pin 2 Black (GND) Spliced from GND Reader ground
Pin 3 Green (D0) RFID D0 wire Wiegand data line 0
Pin 4 White (D1) RFID D1 wire Wiegand data line 1
Pin 5 Yellow (LED/BEEP) LED/BEEP wire Buzzer/light command from ESP32
⚠ RFID Cable Prep

The RFID reader has 6 individual thin wires. Thread adhesive heat-shrink tubing (dual-wall 3:1, 6mm ID) over all 6 wires together and heat — this creates a single round sealed cable that the M25 will clamp over. Prevents water wicking between individual wires. Insulate the 26AWG wire under the shrink.

🔴 Lock Molex Prep

Cut the original white Molex connector from the lock cable. Connect the exposed bare wires to the screw terminals inside the M25 connector body.

05

Sealing Secrets

MARINE ENVIRONMENT — SALT SPRAY RESISTANCE

A. Cable Glands

Drill clean holes and thread glands according to cable type:

  1. PG9 Main cables (UL2464 8-Core 20AWG): Use PG9 glands. Perfect fit for 6.9mm cable diameter.
  2. PG7 Thin cables (ultrasonic sensor eyes): Use PG7 glands in Box 2. Thread the white JST connector through the plastic, then stretch the rubber seal over it.
  3. DC IN Main 12V power input (round plug): Do NOT try to pass the round plug through a gland. Cut the feed cable before the plug, pass the bare wire inside, and connect to a DC Female Screw Terminal Adapter inside the box.

B. Protective Conformal Coating

🛡 Conformal Coating — Spray or Liquid

Spray option: Plastic 70 circuit board lacquer spray — purchase locally in Israel.
Liquid option (may be better & also on AliExpress): Brush-on or dip conformal coating — search "conformal coating liquid PCB" on AliExpress. Liquid gives more controlled application and better penetration into tight spaces. Both protect against salt, moisture and corrosion.
Caution for both: Do NOT apply on USB connectors or screw terminals — only on the board components themselves.

C. External Field Connectors

M25 5-pin waterproof connectors serve as IP68-rated field junctions between each device's short cable and the long AWG20 runs. They sit near each device in the field — not at the box. See Section 04 for full pin assignments.

06

Box Selection & Internal Wiring

POLYCARBONATE ENCLOSURES + INTERNAL TECHNIQUES

Use IP68-rated flame-retardant polycarbonate (PC) black waterproof enclosures. See Bill of Materials for exact part numbers.

A. Internal Connection Techniques

  1. 01 Pin-to-pin (board to board): Use female-to-female Dupont jumper wires. Standard thin wire gauge is sufficient for data signals. Apply a dot of hot glue on the plastic connector after assembly for vibration resistance.
  2. 02 Pin to screw terminal: Cut one end of a Dupont wire (female–female), expose the copper, and clamp directly into the screw terminal block.
  3. 03 Pin to thick external wire (recommended): Cut a Dupont wire leaving only the female head end. Solder the cut end directly to the AWG20 external wire core. Cover the solder joint with adhesive heat-shrink tubing. This creates a super-compact, industrial, vibration-resistant connection. Alternative: use Wago 221 lever connectors — but these take up significantly more space inside the box.

B. Board Mounting & Silica Gel

  1. VHB Board anchoring: Never leave boards loose. Use 3M VHB double-sided tape (thick type) under each board to anchor to the box floor and provide electrical isolation. Leave ~3cm clearance from box walls for gland nuts.
  2. SiO₂ Silica gel desiccant packets (5g each): Place 2 small packets in the bottom of every box. Engineering requirement: glue packets to the plastic floor in an empty corner — prevent them from falling onto circuit boards.
